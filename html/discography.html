Discography

Opus Eponymous

Is the debut studio album of the band. Released on 18 October 2010, with 10 songs, one from the Japanese edition.

"Everything on the first record was about a coming darkness, an impending doom."

Infestissumam

Is the second studio album, released on April 10, 2013. The album is composed of 10 songs, increasing to 16 in its "Redux" version.

"The new album is about something present, and literally the new album deals with the presence of the Antichrist, the Demon."

Meliora

Is the third studio album, released on August 21, 2015. Like the previous album, the standard version has 10 songs while the "redux" has 16.

Lead single "Cirice" won the 2016 Grammy Award for Best Metal Performance

Prequelle

Is the fourth studio album of the band, released on April 13, 2018. Its normal version consists of 10 songs as opposed to its "Exalted" version which has 12 tracks.

"Prequelle" was nominated for the Best Rock Album Grammy Award at the 61st annual awards ceremony.

Impera

Impera is the band's fifth and most recent album, to be released on March 11, 2022, featuring 12 new songs.

The album's theme mainly focuses on "the rise and fall of empires", with sound and melodies reminiscent of 80's rock.

If you have a ghost

Is the band's first EP, releasedon November 20, 2013. Of the 5 songs 4 are covers and the remaining one is a live recording.

This EP was produce by Dave Grohl, best know as the drummer of nirvana and frontman of Foo fighters

Popestar

Popestar is the second EP by the Swedish rock band, released on September 16, 2016.

Exactly as the previous EP, popestar consist almost entirely of covers, being "Square Hammer" the only original track.

Ceremony and Devotion

Compilation album of the most famous songs from his 3 previous productions, being released on October 23, 2017.

Seven Inches of Satan Panic

Is the third EP of the band. It features the songs "Kiss the Go-Goat" and "Mary on a Cross"

Being tracks of the character known as Papa nihil as a celebration of its 50th anniversary
